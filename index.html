Nicolae Paulescu

Famous for his work on diabetes

Here's a time line of Nicolae Paulescu's life:

  • 30 October 1869 - Born in Bucharest, United Principalities of Moldavia and Wallachia
  • 1888 - went to Paris to study medicine, and three years later he took a post as a nonresident medical student at the Hôtel-Dieu de Paris.
  • 1897 - received a degree in medicine.
  • 1898 - doctorates in physiology.
  • 1899 - doctorates in natural sciences
  • 1903 - published with Lancereaux Traité de Médecine, Nosologie (“Treaty of Medicine, Nosology”), the first of a four-volume series on disease and physiology.
  • 1914 - 1916 - Paulescu worked to identify the active pancreatic substance that the earlier research of German physiologist and pathologist Oskar Minkowski and German physician Joseph von Mering had suggested could be used to treat diabetes.
  • 1922 - Paulescu applied for a patent on pancrein and its method of manufacture, and it was awarded by the Minister of Industry and Trade of Romania.
  • 1931 - Dies at the age of 62 in Bucharest - Romania.
